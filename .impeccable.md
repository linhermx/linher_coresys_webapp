## Design Context

### Users
CoreSys is an internal operations product used by Systems/IT operators, requesters, leaders, and non-technical internal profiles. People use it in fast operational contexts to create, review, assign, track, and resolve work with minimal training overhead. The interface must stay understandable without assuming deep technical knowledge and must support immediate scanning, clear status reading, and low-friction actions.

### Brand Personality
Technological, operational, premium. The product should feel controlled, trustworthy, and fast. Its tone should be serious, calm, precise, and polished rather than playful, ornamental, or marketing-heavy. Visual sophistication should come from order, hierarchy, and finish, not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is a modern internal operations UI with compact density, clear hierarchy, and a strong shared system across modules. Light mode is the default experience, with dark mode preserving the same structure and density. Use the existing token foundation: purple as the primary accent, cyan as secondary support, real iconography, and restrained surface treatment. Favor coherent bento-style composition for dashboards when it improves scanability, and support multiple work views such as list, board, and timeline within the same visual language. The current `CS` badge is temporary until an official horizontal logo and monogram are defined.

Reference qualities to preserve:
- coherent visual rhythm across typography, spacing, and color
- dashboards that feel structured and premium without becoming decorative
- operational views that can shift between list, board, and timeline cleanly
- interfaces that communicate purpose through structure instead of explanatory filler copy

Anti-direction:
- generic AI-looking SaaS or fintech dashboards
- excessive card fragmentation
- exaggerated gradients, glow, or visual effects
- redundant blocks that repeat the same information
- screens that need too much descriptive text to explain what they do

### Design Principles
- Make function obvious through structure. A screen should communicate its purpose through layout, hierarchy, state, and actions before long explanatory text is needed.
- Optimize for fast operational scanning. Favor compact density, short labels, visible status, and immediate affordances over decorative whitespace.
- Keep one coherent system across views. Dashboards, tables, boards, timelines, forms, and overlays should feel like variations of the same product, not separate templates.
- Use premium restraint. Quality should come from typography, spacing, balance, and controlled color, not from visual excess.
- Design for mixed technical depth. Interfaces must work equally well for Systems operators and non-technical internal users while meeting WCAG AA accessibility expectations.
