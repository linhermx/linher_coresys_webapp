# Impeccable Context

## Design Context

### Users

Coresys is used by Sistemas/IT operators as the primary operational audience and by common internal users who mainly create and follow up on tickets.

The main job is to manage, assign and track Sistemas/IT resources, access media and support requests in one traceable operational flow. The interface must support real work: ticket intake, assignment, follow-up, inventory control, access management, service tracking, timelines, activity history, filters and multi-view exploration.

### Brand Personality

The product should feel operativa, precisa and estética.

The intended emotional response is control, clarity and operational speed. Users should feel that the system helps them understand what is happening, what changed, what needs attention and what action comes next without visual noise or artificial complexity.

### Aesthetic Direction

The visual direction is sober, technical and premium, with modern accents. The default theme is light mode, but light and dark modes must both be designed from the beginning.

Relevant product references are Jira, Monday and ClickUp, not as visual clones but as operational references:
- multi-view data exploration through grids, pipelines, timelines and lists
- strong filtering and sorting patterns
- careful topbar and sidebar hierarchy
- dense but readable operational layouts
- precise placement of actions, metadata and status indicators
- UX that makes complex workflows feel intuitive

Local visual references live in `frontend/references/` and should be reviewed when interface work begins.

Avoid generic AI-made SaaS aesthetics: excessive gradients, ornamental dashboards, saturated layouts, decorative cards without operational logic and artificial compositions that do not help the workflow.

Use purple as a controlled accent color, never as a loud purple-blue gradient identity. Use additional colors where they add meaning, such as statuses and indicators, but keep them calm and comfortable for sustained work.

### Design Principles

1. Design for operations first: every visual decision must make tracking, assignment, filtering, status review or action-taking clearer.
2. Keep density compact but legible: support real Sistemas/IT workflows without turning screens into saturated dashboards.
3. Use hierarchy with precision: sidebar, topbar, filters, tables, timelines and actions must have deliberate placement and clear priority.
4. Treat color as information: purple is an accent, status colors must be restrained, and no palette should strain the user's eyes.
5. Build for consistency across views: grids, pipelines, timelines and lists should feel like different lenses on the same operational domain.
6. Preserve accessibility and theme quality: WCAG AA remains the baseline, with light as default and dark mode designed intentionally from the start.
